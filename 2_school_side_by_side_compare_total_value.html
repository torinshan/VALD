üìö Methodology & Data Sources
üîç Data Collection

Benefits data was systematically researched through:

  • Official university HR websites
  • State employee benefits systems (PEBA, PEBP, SEGIP, etc.)
  • Retirement system documentation (TRS, OPERS, TIAA)
  • University benefits guides and PDFs
üèõÔ∏è Tax Calculations

Tax computations use official 2025 rates:

  • Federal: 7-bracket progressive system
  • State: All 50 states + DC rates
  • FICA: 6.2% SS (up to $168,600) + 1.45% Medicare
  • Local taxes where applicable
üìç Cost of Living

COL indices mapped to school locations:

  • 42,000+ ZIP codes indexed
  • National average baseline = 100
  • Components: Housing, Grocery, Utilities, Transportation, Health

This data was searched and compiled with AI assistance and may contain errors.

This tool is designed to provide approximations to assist in the decision-making process. It is NOT intended to be a definitive source for employment decisions.

Before making any employment decisions, you should:

  • Verify benefits information directly with the institution's HR department
  • Request official benefits documentation
  • Consult with a financial advisor or tax professional
  • Consider factors not captured in this tool (job satisfaction, career growth, family needs, etc.)

Known Limitations: Benefits data reflects publicly available information at time of research. Some institutions have "Estimated" data quality ratings. Tiered premium systems may not be fully modeled. IRS contribution limits not enforced.
